A Tribute to Dr. Akkineni Nageswara Rao

The Doyen of Telugu Cinema

Akkineni Nageswara Rao (ANR) (1923–2014) was a legendary Indian actor and producer, revered as a pillar of Telugu cinema for over seven decades. Known as "Natasamrat," he starred in over 255 films—spanning Telugu, Tamil, and Hindi—and was a pioneer in moving the Telugu film industry to Hyderabad, establishing Annapurna Studios in 1975.

Birth: Born on September 20, 1923, in Venkataraghavapuram, Andhra Pradesh.
Debut: Started his career in theater, often playing female roles, before his film debut in Dharmapatni (1941).
Rise to Stardom: Gained massive popularity with films like Balaraju (1948) and the iconic Devadasu (1953), which solidified his image as the "Tragic Hero".
Versatility: He was known for playing diverse roles, including mythological figures, poets, and lovers, such as in Vipra Narayana (1954), Tenali Ramakrishna (1956), and Amarasilpi Jakkanna (1964).


A legendary actor whose career spanned over 75 years

Family: Married Annapurna Akkineni. His son, Akkineni Nagarjuna, is a prominent actor, as are his grandsons Sumanth, Sushanth, and Naga Chaitanya.
Death: Passed away on January 22, 2014, after a battle with cancer.
Legacy: Known for his discipline, he was a prolific writer, including his autobiography Nenu Na Jeevitham.

Famous Works: Laila Majnu (1949), Anarkali (1955), Prem Nagar (1971), Premabhishekam (1981), and Meghasandesam (1983).
Final Film: He acted alongside his son Nagarjuna and grandson Naga Chaitanya in the 2014 film Manam, which was released posthumously.
Contribution: Founded Annapurna Studios (1975) and the Annapurna International School of Film and Media (2011), shaping the infrastructure of Telugu cinema


Awards and Honors:

Padma Vibhushan (2011): India's second-highest civilian award.
Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1990): Highest award for cinematic excellence in India.
Padma Bhushan (1988) & Padma Shri (1968).
Nandi Awards: Won several Best Actor awards, including for Doctor Chakravarthy and Sudigundalu.
